RED BULL RACING

Red Bull Racing is one of the most iconic and successful teams in Formula 1, famous for its combination of fearless racing, bold design, and relentless pursuit of innovation. Founded in 2005 and based in Milton Keynes, the team quickly transformed from newcomers into champions, winning four consecutive Drivers’ and Constructors’ Championships with Sebastian Vettel between 2010 and 2013. In recent years, Max Verstappen has carried the team into a new era of dominance, securing multiple world titles and establishing himself as one of the greatest drivers of the generation. Supported by strong engineering, clever race strategies, and the powerful partnership with Oracle that brings advanced cloud computing and AI into their operations, Red Bull has redefined how technology and racing work hand in hand. Their car development is led by world-class technical expertise, with Red Bull Powertrains preparing for the revolutionary 2026 engine regulations. Alongside their racing success, the team is also known for nurturing young talent through the Red Bull Junior Program, ensuring a steady pipeline of drivers ready to take on the biggest stage. With their trademark aggressive style, thrilling overtakes, and a culture built around energy and innovation, Red Bull Racing continues to capture the imagination of fans worldwide and stands as the benchmark of modern Formula 1 excellence.



MERCEDES-AMG PETRONAS

Mercedes-AMG Petronas Formula 1 Team is one of the most dominant and respected forces in the history of the sport, combining German engineering excellence with a culture of precision, teamwork, and innovation. Based in Brackley, United Kingdom, and backed by Mercedes-Benz, the team began its modern F1 journey in 2010 and quickly rose to become a powerhouse. From 2014 to 2020, Mercedes ushered in the turbo-hybrid era with unmatched dominance, winning seven consecutive Constructors’ Championships and helping Lewis Hamilton secure six of his seven world titles. Alongside Hamilton, drivers like Nico Rosberg, who won the 2016 Championship, and George Russell, who represents the team’s future, have showcased Mercedes’ ability to balance experience with youth. The team’s success has been built on cutting-edge car design, world-class aerodynamics, and powerful engines developed at Mercedes’ High Performance Powertrains facility in Brixworth. Beyond the track, Mercedes has also embraced sustainability, committing to reducing carbon emissions and leading Formula 1 into a greener future. Known for their iconic silver and black liveries, incredible consistency, and reputation for operational perfection, Mercedes remains a symbol of excellence and continues to push the boundaries of performance, technology, and innovation in Formula 1.

SCUDERIA FERRARI

Scuderia Ferrari is the oldest and most iconic team in Formula 1, carrying a legacy that stretches back to the very beginning of the championship in 1950. Based in Maranello, Italy, Ferrari is more than just a racing team—it is a symbol of passion, tradition, and Italian excellence. Over the decades, Ferrari has claimed multiple Constructors’ and Drivers’ Championships, with legends like Niki Lauda, Michael Schumacher, and Kimi Räikkönen writing their names into the history books under the Prancing Horse banner. Known for their striking red cars, the team has built an emotional bond with fans worldwide, proudly supported by the famous “Tifosi” who bring unmatched energy to every race. In the modern era, drivers Charles Leclerc and Carlos Sainz are leading Ferrari’s charge to return to championship glory, supported by a strong engineering team focused on innovation, aerodynamics, and engine development. Beyond racing, Ferrari represents heritage and prestige, blending its motorsport success with its reputation as one of the most admired car manufacturers in the world. Despite fierce competition, Scuderia Ferrari continues to stand as the heart and soul of Formula 1, embodying both its history and its future.


MCLAREN F1 TEAM

McLaren is one of the most legendary names in Formula 1, blending a rich history of success with a forward-looking vision for innovation and performance. Founded in 1963 by Bruce McLaren, the team is based in Woking, United Kingdom, and has grown into one of the most successful teams in F1 history. Over the years, McLaren has won multiple Constructors’ and Drivers’ Championships, with greats such as Ayrton Senna, Alain Prost, Niki Lauda, and Lewis Hamilton delivering unforgettable victories in the team’s famous papaya and silver cars. Known for their engineering creativity and competitive spirit, McLaren has consistently pushed boundaries both on and off the track. In recent years, with drivers like Lando Norris and Oscar Piastri leading the charge, McLaren has been on a resurgence, regularly challenging for podiums and re-establishing itself as a force to be reckoned with. Beyond racing, McLaren is also synonymous with cutting-edge technology, innovation in automotive engineering, and a passionate fan base that has stood by the team through highs and lows. With its iconic papaya orange livery, a proud racing legacy, and a strong focus on the future, McLaren remains one of the most respected and exciting teams in Formula 1.


ALPINE

Alpine is a relatively young name in Formula 1 but carries the proud legacy of Renault, one of the sport’s historic manufacturers. Based in Enstone, United Kingdom, with power unit facilities in Viry-Châtillon, France, the team was rebranded as Alpine in 2021 to showcase Renault’s performance brand and its commitment to innovation and electrification. Over the years, under different names, the team has celebrated great successes, including back-to-back Drivers’ and Constructors’ Championships in 2005 and 2006 with Fernando Alonso, marking Renault’s golden era in F1. With its striking blue livery inspired by French racing heritage, Alpine has carved out its own identity, focusing on steady progress, strong midfield battles, and building towards future success. Drivers like Esteban Ocon, who delivered Alpine’s first F1 win in Hungary 2021, and Pierre Gasly, add to the team’s all-French lineup and bring both talent and national pride. Though not yet a consistent front-runner, Alpine’s combination of experienced engineering, passionate ambition, and long-term vision makes it a team with exciting potential. Representing France on the global stage, Alpine continues to fight with determination, aiming to close the gap to Formula 1’s front-running giants.

STAKE KICK SAUBER

Stake F1 Team Kick Sauber is one of the newest identities on the Formula 1 grid, yet it carries decades of racing heritage through its roots as Sauber Motorsport. Based in Hinwil, Switzerland, the team first entered Formula 1 in 1993 and has since built a reputation for precision engineering and nurturing young talent, with names like Kimi Räikkönen, Felipe Massa, and Sebastian Vettel starting their careers under its wing. After years as Alfa Romeo’s works team, Sauber began a new chapter in 2024 under the Stake and Kick partnership, blending motorsport tradition with bold, modern branding. While currently competing as an independent entry, the team is also preparing for a landmark future as it transitions into the Audi works team in 2026, marking one of the most exciting projects in F1. With drivers like Valtteri Bottas and Zhou Guanyu, Stake F1 Team Kick Sauber continues to fight in the competitive midfield, combining experience with youth and maintaining a spirit of determination. Known for its Swiss efficiency, innovative design, and resilience in the face of challenges, the team is steadily laying the foundation for a return to the front of the grid. Though often considered an underdog, Stake Kick Sauber embodies the passion and grit that keeps Formula 1 unpredictable and thrilling.

HAAS F1 TEAM

Haas F1 Team is the first American-led outfit to compete in Formula 1 in decades, bringing a fresh perspective to the sport since its debut in 2016. Founded by businessman Gene Haas, who also owns a successful NASCAR team, Haas F1 is based in Kannapolis, North Carolina, with its main operations in Banbury, UK, and chassis support from Dallara in Italy. The team entered the sport with a bold, cost-efficient model, relying on close technical partnerships with Ferrari for power units and other components, allowing them to be competitive right from their debut season. Over the years, Haas has experienced both strong points finishes and tough rebuilding seasons, but it has always remained a popular underdog on the grid. Known for its no-nonsense approach, striking black, white, and red liveries, and straightforward leadership style, Haas has carved out its own identity in Formula 1. With drivers like Nico Hülkenberg and Kevin Magnussen, the team blends experience, consistency, and resilience as it continues to fight in the highly competitive midfield. While still chasing its first podium, Haas embodies determination, adaptability, and the spirit of pushing forward—qualities that make it an exciting team to watch as it works toward bigger ambitions in the sport.

ASTON MARTIN COGNIZANT

Aston Martin Aramco Formula 1 Team is one of the most ambitious and rapidly developing outfits on the current F1 grid, combining British heritage with a bold vision for the future. Based at Silverstone in the United Kingdom, the team was rebranded as Aston Martin in 2021 after billionaire Lawrence Stroll acquired the Racing Point outfit and brought back one of the most iconic names in motorsport. Known for their striking green livery inspired by Aston Martin’s legendary racing history, the team has quickly become a fan favorite. With four-time World Champion Sebastian Vettel helping lay the foundations before his retirement, the team now relies on the experience of Fernando Alonso and the youth of Lance Stroll to lead its charge. Aston Martin has invested heavily in new state-of-the-art facilities, wind tunnels, and talent recruitment, signaling its determination to compete at the very front of the field in the coming years. Their strong 2023 season, which included multiple podiums, showed that the Silverstone-based squad can challenge established giants like Mercedes, Ferrari, and Red Bull. Backed by technical partnerships and a long-term vision, Aston Martin Aramco F1 is not just reviving a legendary name but also shaping itself into a serious contender for future championships


WILLIAMS RACING

Williams Racing is one of the most historic and beloved teams in Formula 1, carrying a legacy of passion, engineering brilliance, and championship success. Founded in 1977 by Sir Frank Williams and Patrick Head, the team quickly rose to prominence, becoming one of the sport’s dominant forces during the 1980s and 1990s. With nine Constructors’ Championships and seven Drivers’ Championships, Williams has worked with legends such as Nigel Mansell, Alain Prost, Nelson Piquet, Damon Hill, and Jacques Villeneuve, etching its name firmly into F1 history. Based in Grove, Oxfordshire, the team is renowned for its independent spirit and resilience, often achieving incredible results despite competing against far larger manufacturers. In recent years, Williams has undergone a rebuilding phase under new ownership and management, with a strong focus on modernizing facilities, investing in young talent, and restoring its competitive edge. Drivers like Alex Albon and Logan Sargeant represent the team’s new era, bringing both experience and fresh energy to the grid. Known for its iconic blue-and-white liveries and its loyal fanbase, Williams Racing remains a symbol of tradition, perseverance, and the enduring spirit of competition in Formula 1.

VISA RACING BULLS

The Visa Cash App RB Formula One Team, often referred to as Racing Bulls, is the rebranded identity of the Faenza-based squad formerly known as Scuderia AlphaTauri and Toro Rosso. Acting as the sister team to Red Bull Racing, it has long been a proving ground for young talent, helping launch the careers of drivers like Sebastian Vettel, Daniel Ricciardo, Max Verstappen, Pierre Gasly, and Carlos Sainz. Founded in 2006 as Toro Rosso, the team has enjoyed memorable moments, including Vettel’s stunning maiden win at Monza in 2008 and Gasly’s emotional victory at the same track in 2020. In 2024, the team entered a bold new chapter under the Visa and Cash App sponsorships, adopting a refreshed image, striking blue-and-red livery, and a stronger technical integration with Red Bull Technology to improve its competitiveness. With drivers like Yuki Tsunoda and Daniel Ricciardo providing a mix of youthful energy and veteran experience, Racing Bulls aims to push further up the midfield battle while continuing its mission of developing the next generation of Red Bull talent. Combining Italian passion, Red Bull’s support, and an eye on future success, the Visa Cash App RB team remains one of the most exciting and dynamic squads on the grid.
